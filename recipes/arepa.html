Arepa

Description

Arepas are an all-in-one filler. They are made of pre-cooked cornmeal, beans, salty cheese, platains, and beef.

If you eat one, rest assured you will feel filled up for the rest of the day. That's how tasty and nutritious they are.

Ingredients

  • 2 ½ cups lukewarm water
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 cups pre-cooked white cornmeal (such as P.A.N.®)
  • ¼ cup vegetable oil, or as needed
  • A can of black beans
  • About a pound of beef
  • 1 plantain

Steps

  1. Stir water and salt together in a medium bowl; gradually stir in cornmeal with your fingers until the mixture forms a soft, moist, malleable dough.
  2. Form dough into eight 2-inch diameter balls; pat each ball to flatten into a 3/8-inch-thick arepa patty.
  3. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium heat; fry arepas in batches until golden brown, about 4 to 5 minutes per side.
  4. Transfer cooked arepas to a paper towel-lined plate to drain until cool enough to handle.
  5. Slice halfway through each arepa horizontally with a thin serrated knife to form a pita-like pocket.
  6. Get ready to put your beef on a separate pan. Let it heat for a few minutes. If you add a few drops of water, you will know the pan is ready when the water makes an air bubble that bounces around.
  7. Once the pan is hot, add your beef to it. Cook for about 4-5 minutes.
  8. Shred your beef either by slicing it up in long, thin chunks or by using a shredder.
  9. Grab a can of black beans and heat it up on a pan for about 5 minutes.
  10. If you want to add plantains, you can slice them up at an angle and fry them up in oil until they turn golden.
  11. With the arepa now sliced up, you can fill it up with the shredded beef, beans, mozzarella cheese, and fried plantains.
